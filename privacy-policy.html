Privacy Policy for Kudoro

Effective date: 29.06.2025

Introduction

This Privacy Policy applies to the Kudoro app, which is developed and maintained by Timon Lapawczyk. Kudoro does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data from users. The app is provided “as is” and used entirely at your own risk. For questions or concerns, please contact kudoro.cc@gmail.com.

What information does Kudoro obtain and how is it used?

Kudoro does not collect or store any personal data on its own. Strava activity data is only accessed if you connect your Strava account, and it is processed entirely on your device.

Data from Strava

Strava data is accessed through the official Strava API, using explicit authorization.

Kudoro has access to both public and private activities from your Strava account.

This data is processed entirely on your device and is never uploaded to Kudoro servers or shared with third parties.

It is in the nature of the generated overlays that they include activity data. These overlays are stored only on your device. Choosing who to share them with is entirely up to you - for example, through social media, messaging apps, or any other method.

Access to Strava can be revoked at any time through Kudoro’s settings.

Does Kudoro collect precise real-time location information?

Kudoro does not collect precise information about the location of your device.

Do third parties see and/or have access to information obtained by Kudoro?

Kudoro does not knowingly collect or share any personal data. However, some third-party libraries used by Kudoro may collect certain data automatically as part of their normal operation (e.g., crash reporting or analytics). No such data is shared by Kudoro with any third parties.

For details on third-party technologies used, please see the Third-Party Technologies section below.

Third-Party Technologies

Kudoro uses certain third-party libraries and technologies to provide its features. Information about these third-party technologies can be found in the About section of the app.

What are opt-out rights?

All data collection can be stopped by uninstalling Kudoro using the standard uninstall processes available as part of your device or app store.

Children’s Privacy

Kudoro does not knowingly collect any personal or identifying information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

If a parent or guardian believes a child has submitted personal information, please contact kudoro.cc@gmail.com to request deletion.

Security

Since Kudoro does not collect any information, there is no risk of data being accessed by unauthorized individuals.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

This privacy policy may be updated from time to time. The most current version will always be available at https://kudoro.cc/privacy-policy.

Consult this Privacy Policy regularly, as continued use of Kudoro constitutes acceptance of any changes.

Consent

Using Kudoro constitutes consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

Contact

For privacy questions or concerns, contact kudoro.cc@gmail.com.
